  2. Matters concerning the installation, operation, and refusal of automatic personal information collection devices

    1. Guides on the use of Cookie for membership

      1. What is a cookie? The Company uses and saves cookies in the member authentication section where you log in to provide a customized service for you.
        Cookies are text files that are automatically generated in your web browser when you access the website. This cookie will be automatically deleted when you log out (Log-out) and close your web browser.
      2. Purpose of use Cookies store and utilize your visit and usage behavior of the Hancom services and websites, IP address, browser type, connection server, server location, and response to certain ads. When a customized service is provided, user's personal information is not provided.
      3. How to Refuse to Save Cookies Users reserve the rights to choose to install cookies. Users can adjust web browser settings to accept or refuse cookies. However, refusing to save cookies may limit the use of some services.
      1. How to access cookie settings in Internet Explorer * Tools → Internet Options → Privacy → Advanced → Cookies in Settings
      2. How to access cookie settings in Microsoft Edge * At the top of the web browser, select [Settings and more] → [Settings] → [Site permissions] → [Cookies and site data]
      3. How to access cookie settings in Chrome * At the top of the web browser, click on the three dots and [Settings] → [Privacy and security] → [Cookies and other site data]

    2. Guides on Google Analytics

      1. The Company uses Google Analytics, a web analytics service provided by Google LLC (hereinafter referred to as "Google") to better serve customers. Through this, we analyze how you use the service and identify demand to improve the service and product.
      2. Google Analytics uses the Cookie key to collect how you use your website.
      3. Google sends and stores information collected through cookies to Google servers in the United States.
      4. Google may provide this information to third parties, if required by law, or to third parties that process it on behalf of Google.
      5. Google does not associate your IP address with any other data it has.
      6. Unless you specifically refuse to use cookies, you agree to use all information generated through Google Analytics and cookies created during your company's service use.
      7. Information on Google's privacy can be found here.
      8. If you want to refuse data collection by Google Analytics, you can either set it up on Google's site or refuse to save cookies via [How to Refuse to Save Cookies] above. However, please note that refusing to save cookies may limit the use of some services that require login, and that you are solely responsible for them.

    3. Behavior information

      1. Microsoft Clarity

        Microsoft Clarity is a user behavior analytics tool that helps understand how users are interacting with a website through session replays and heatmaps.
        Microsoft Clarity uses Cookies to collect how users use the website. If the user wants to refuse data collection, he/she can either set it up on Microsoft Clarity's site or refuse to save cookies via [How to Refuse to Save Cookies] above. However, please note that refusing to save cookies may limit the use of some services that require login, and that you are solely responsible for them. Information on Microsoft's privacy can be found here.
